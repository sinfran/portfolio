Developing Design Guidelines for AR-Based Assembly Instructions


Collaborators

Janet Chen
Dongwook Yoon
Laura Herman (Adobe Research)
Cuong Nguyen (Adobe Research)

Summary

In the past, several studies have described the capabilities and benefits of AR in the field of assembly. Unlike traditional assembly instructions, which are illustrated and printed in a paper manual, AR can provide instructions in an interactive manner by overlaying virtual content on top of the real world.




Although in-situ spatial guidance has been shown to be more effective than traditional 2D instruction manuals for physical assembly tasks, the literature is lacking in a well-established set of design guidelines (i.e., specific and actionable design suggestions that practitioners can adopt for real-world applications) for AR-based instructions. The core methodological challenge in developing guidelines for a cutting-edge technology like AR is the scarcity of reliable information resources. To address this issue, we developed a mixed-methods pipeline. The process begins with identifying design patterns by analyzing easy-to-access, but perhaps noisy, datasets. Then, it progressively transforms the patterns into guidelines by triangulating more reliable, but scarce, data points (e.g., peer-reviewed literature, expert input).
